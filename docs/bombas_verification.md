# Bombas Verification

## Finding

Bombas is a legitimate Shark Tank win for Daymond John, but it was not Daymond John's own founded company.

## Evidence

- Bombas' own timeline says Bombas appeared on Shark Tank in 2014 and won an investment from Daymond John: https://bombas.com/pages/about-us
- Business Insider identifies Randy Goldberg and David Heath as the cofounders and reports that they landed a Daymond John deal for `$200,000` in exchange for `17.5%` of the company plus inventory financing: https://www.businessinsider.com/shark-tank-bombas-rehearsed-answers-to-all-potential-questions-2018-9
- Entrepreneur likewise describes Goldberg and Heath as cofounders and reports a Daymond John deal for `$200,000` and `17.5%` equity: https://www.entrepreneur.com/starting-a-business/bombas-is-the-most-successful-shark-tank-brand-heres/464309
- Investopedia lists Bombas as the highest-selling Shark Tank product, says Daymond John invested `$200,000` for a `17.5%` stake, and reports that Bombas surpassed `$2B` in sales in 2025: https://www.investopedia.com/articles/investing/082415/10-most-successful-products-shark-tank.asp

## Scoring Implication

The original MVP attributed full Bombas lifetime sales to Daymond John, which overstated direct investor economics. The dashboard now keeps full portfolio-company revenue for context but uses ownership-weighted attributed revenue for Alpha Score when equity can be parsed from deal terms.

For Bombas:

- Portfolio-company revenue in the dataset: `$2B+ lifetime sales`
- Reported Daymond stake: `17.5%`
- Attributed revenue used for Alpha Score: `$350M`

This is still a major win, but it is no longer counted as if Daymond owned 100% of Bombas.
